Kumbhalgarh Fort

The Great Wall of India & Birthplace of Maharana Pratap

Introduction

Kumbhalgarh Fort is one of the most magnificent forts in Rajasthan and is famous for having the second longest wall in the world after the Great Wall of China.

Located in the Aravalli Hills, this UNESCO World Heritage Site stands as a powerful symbol of Rajput strength and architectural brilliance.

History of Kumbhalgarh Fort

The fort was built in the 15th century by Rana Kumbha, one of the greatest rulers of Mewar. It took over 15 years to complete.

Kumbhalgarh is also the birthplace of Maharana Pratap, one of India's most respected Rajput warriors.

The fort remained unconquered for centuries due to its strong defenses and strategic hilltop location.

The Great Wall of India

Kumbhalgarh Fort is famous for its massive wall that stretches over 36 kilometers.

The wall is wide enough for eight horses to walk side by side, earning it the title "The Great Wall of India."

Architecture & Important Structures

The fort complex includes:

  • Badal Mahal (Palace of Clouds)
  • Over 300 Jain and Hindu Temples
  • Massive Gates (Pols)
  • Watch Towers & Defensive Walls

The design showcases traditional Rajput architecture combined with military strategy.

Travel Guide

  • Location: Rajsamand District, Rajasthan
  • Timings: 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹40 (Indian), ₹600 (Foreigners)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March
  • Nearest Airport: Udaipur Airport
