Amber Fort Jaipur

The Magnificent Rajput Palace of Rajasthan

Introduction

Amber Fort, also known as Amer Fort, is one of the most magnificent historical monuments in Rajasthan. Located about 11 kilometres from Jaipur city, this grand fort stands proudly on a hill overlooking the beautiful Maota Lake. Built with red sandstone and white marble, Amber Fort reflects the royal lifestyle and architectural brilliance of Rajput rulers.

Every year, thousands of tourists from across India and around the world visit this monument to explore its history, admire its architecture and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding Aravalli hills.

History of Amber Fort

Amber Fort was constructed in 1592 by Raja Man Singh I, one of the most trusted generals of Mughal Emperor Akbar. The fort later underwent expansions under Mirza Raja Jai Singh I and Sawai Jai Singh II. Before Jaipur city was established in 1727, Amber served as the capital of the Kachwaha Rajputs.

The fort witnessed several political alliances and royal ceremonies. Its strategic hilltop location made it an ideal defensive structure during medieval times.

Architecture of Amber Fort

The architecture of Amber Fort beautifully combines Rajput and Mughal styles. While the outer structure appears strong and defensive, the inner palaces reflect luxury and artistic excellence.

Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace)

The Sheesh Mahal is one of the most famous attractions of Amber Fort. Thousands of small mirrors decorate the walls and ceilings, creating magical reflections when light enters the room.

Diwan-e-Aam

This was the Hall of Public Audience where the king addressed common people and heard their grievances.

Diwan-e-Khas

The Hall of Private Audience was used for royal meetings and discussions.

Scenic Beauty and Surroundings

The fort overlooks Maota Lake which enhances its visual beauty. The reflection of the fort in the lake during sunrise and sunset creates a spectacular view. The surrounding Aravalli hills provide a natural defensive barrier and scenic charm.

Light and Sound Show

Amber Fort hosts a light and sound show in the evening that narrates the glorious history of Jaipur and the Rajput rulers. This show is available in both Hindi and English languages and is highly recommended for visitors.

Travel Information

  • Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan
  • Timings: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM
  • Light Show: Evening
  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹100 (Indian Visitors)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March

Travel Tips

  • Visit early in the morning to avoid heavy crowds.
  • Wear comfortable footwear as there is walking involved.
  • Carry water during summer season.
  • Hire a local guide for deeper historical understanding.

Conclusion

Amber Fort is not just a historical monument; it is a symbol of Rajasthan’s royal heritage. Its grand architecture, cultural importance and scenic beauty make it one of the must-visit destinations in India. Whether you are a history lover, architecture enthusiast or travel photographer, Amber Fort offers a memorable experience.
