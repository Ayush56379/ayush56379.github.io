Amber Fort Jaipur

The Magnificent Rajput Palace of Rajasthan

Introduction

Amber Fort, also known as Amer Fort, is one of the most magnificent historical monuments in Rajasthan. Located about 11 kilometres from Jaipur city, the fort stands on a hill overlooking the scenic Maota Lake. Built using red sandstone and white marble, it represents the power and luxury of Rajput rulers. Today it is recognised as part of the UNESCO World Heritage Hill Forts of Rajasthan.

History of Amber Fort

The construction of Amber Fort began in 1592 under Raja Man Singh I, a trusted general of Mughal Emperor Akbar. Later rulers expanded the complex with royal halls, gardens and temples. Before Jaipur city was founded, Amber served as the capital of the Kachwaha Rajputs. The fort witnessed political alliances, royal ceremonies and important historical events.

Architecture

Amber Fort combines Rajput military architecture with Mughal artistic elegance. Famous sections include the Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), Diwan-e-Aam and Diwan-e-Khas. Thousands of mirrors decorate the palace interiors, creating magical reflections when light enters the hall. Massive gates and defensive walls protected the fort, while inner courtyards provided comfort and privacy for royal families.
