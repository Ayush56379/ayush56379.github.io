Agra Fort

The Powerful Mughal Stronghold of India

Introduction

Agra Fort is one of the most important Mughal forts in India and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, this massive red sandstone fort served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors.

Built in the 16th century, Agra Fort represents the peak of Mughal military strength, architectural brilliance, and royal lifestyle.

History of Agra Fort

The present structure of Agra Fort was built by Emperor Akbar in 1565. Later emperors like Jahangir and Shah Jahan added marble palaces and beautiful courtyards.

Shah Jahan, the builder of the Taj Mahal, was imprisoned here by his son Aurangzeb. From the Musamman Burj tower, he spent his final years looking at the Taj Mahal across the Yamuna River.

The fort remained the Mughal capital until 1638 when the capital shifted to Delhi.

Architecture

Agra Fort is built primarily with red sandstone. Later structures added by Shah Jahan include white marble elements.

The fort features massive defensive walls, elegant palaces, courtyards, mosques, and royal chambers.

  • Jahangir Palace
  • Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience)
  • Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience)
  • Musamman Burj
  • Moti Masjid

Importance

Agra Fort is not only a military structure but also a royal city within walls. It shows how Mughal emperors balanced security, luxury, and artistic expression.

Its strategic location near the Yamuna River made it one of the strongest forts in medieval India.

Travel Guide

  • Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh
  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site
  • Timings: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹50 (Indian), ₹650 (Foreigners)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March
  • Nearby Attraction: Taj Mahal (2.5 km away)
