Khajuraho Temples

The Iconic UNESCO World Heritage Temple Complex

Introduction

Khajuraho Temples, located in Madhya Pradesh, are one of India’s most famous UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Built between 950 and 1050 AD by the Chandela dynasty, these temples are known for their extraordinary sculptures and architectural brilliance.

Originally around 85 temples were constructed, but today about 20 temples survive, showcasing India's rich artistic heritage.

Historical Background

The temples were built during the reign of the Chandela rulers, who promoted art, culture, and spirituality. The name Khajuraho is believed to be derived from the word “Khajur” meaning date palm trees.

These temples were forgotten for centuries and rediscovered in the 19th century by British engineer T.S. Burt.

Architecture & Sculptures

Khajuraho temples are built in Nagara-style architecture. They feature tall spires (shikharas), intricately carved walls, and symbolic sculptures.

The sculptures depict various themes including daily life, gods and goddesses, dance, music, and divine love. The temples are divided into three groups: Western, Eastern, and Southern groups.

Main Temples to Visit

  • Kandariya Mahadev Temple – Largest and most ornate temple
  • Lakshmana Temple – Dedicated to Lord Vishnu
  • Vishvanatha Temple – Dedicated to Lord Shiva
  • Chitragupta Temple – Dedicated to Sun God
