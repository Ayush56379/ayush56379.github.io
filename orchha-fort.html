Indian Palaces

Orchha Fort

The Grand Palace Complex of the Bundela Dynasty

Introduction

Orchha Fort is a stunning palace complex located in Orchha, Madhya Pradesh. Built in the 16th century by the Bundela Rajputs, this fort complex reflects royal grandeur and rich cultural heritage.

Situated on the banks of the Betwa River, Orchha Fort is known for its beautiful palaces, temples, and chhatris.

Historical Background

The fort complex was founded by Raja Rudra Pratap Singh in 1531. Later rulers expanded the complex by building grand structures like Jahangir Mahal and Raja Mahal.

Jahangir Mahal was specially constructed to welcome Mughal Emperor Jahangir during his visit to Orchha.

Major Attractions Inside Orchha Fort

  • Jahangir Mahal: A Mughal-Rajput architectural masterpiece.
  • Raja Mahal: Famous for beautiful murals and paintings.
  • Rai Praveen Mahal: Dedicated to poet and musician Rai Praveen.
  • Royal Chhatris: Cenotaphs along the Betwa River.

The architecture combines Rajput design with Mughal influence, creating a unique visual experience.

Architecture & Design

Orchha Fort features large courtyards, domes, balconies, and intricately decorated walls. The palaces are built using sandstone and lime mortar.

The murals inside Raja Mahal depict religious themes and royal life scenes.

Travel Guide

  • Location: Orchha, Madhya Pradesh
  • Timings: 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹40 (Indian), ₹600 (Foreign)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March
  • Nearest Railway Station: Jhansi (18 km)
