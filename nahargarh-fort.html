Nahargarh Fort

The Scenic Hill Fort Overlooking Jaipur

Introduction

Nahargarh Fort is one of the most beautiful hill forts in Jaipur, Rajasthan. Situated on the Aravalli hills, it offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Pink City. The fort was originally built as a defense structure but later became a royal retreat.

History of Nahargarh Fort

Nahargarh Fort was built in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the founder of Jaipur. Originally named Sudarshangarh, it was later renamed Nahargarh, meaning “Abode of Tigers.”

According to local legend, the spirit of a prince named Nahar Singh Bhomia haunted the construction site. To appease him, a temple was built within the fort premises.

Architecture & Madhavendra Bhawan

One of the most fascinating parts of Nahargarh Fort is Madhavendra Bhawan, built by Maharaja Sawai Madho Singh. It consists of suites for the king and his queens, connected by corridors. The architecture reflects Rajput style with beautiful frescoes and decorative interiors.

Sunset Views & Tourism

Nahargarh Fort is famous for its stunning sunset views. Tourists visit in the evening to enjoy the panoramic cityscape of Jaipur illuminated with lights.

The fort also houses a wax museum and sculpture park, making it a complete tourist attraction.

Travel Guide

  • Location: Jaipur, Rajasthan
  • Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:30 PM
  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹100 (Indian), ₹300 (Foreigners)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March
  • Nearby Attractions: Amber Fort, Jaigarh Fort
