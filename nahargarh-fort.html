About Nahargarh Fort

Nahargarh Fort stands on the Aravalli hills overlooking Jaipur city. It forms part of Jaipur’s strong defense system along with Amber Fort and Jaigarh Fort.

History

The fort was built in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II. It was originally named Sudarshangarh but later became known as Nahargarh, meaning “Abode of Tigers.”

Architecture

The fort features long corridors, beautiful rooms, and Madhavendra Bhawan — a palace built for the king and his queens with identical suites.

Scenic Views

Nahargarh Fort is famous for offering breathtaking sunset views over Jaipur city, making it a popular tourist attraction.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit is October to March when the weather is pleasant.

Entry Fee & Timings

Indian Visitors: ₹50 Foreign Visitors: ₹200 Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:30 PM (Open daily)
