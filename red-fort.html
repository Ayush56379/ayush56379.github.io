About Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is one of the most iconic historical monuments in India. Located in Old Delhi, it was built in 1648 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan when he shifted his capital from Agra to Delhi. The fort served as the main residence of Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years.

History

Constructed using red sandstone, the Red Fort became the political and ceremonial center of the Mughal Empire. After India’s independence in 1947, the Prime Minister of India hoists the national flag here every Independence Day.

Architecture

The fort complex covers around 254 acres and showcases Indo-Islamic architecture. Major attractions inside include:

  • Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience)
  • Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience)
  • Rang Mahal
  • Jama Masjid

Best Time to Visit

October to March is the best time to visit Red Fort due to pleasant weather conditions.

Entry Fee & Timings
