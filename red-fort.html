The Red Fort, or Lal Qila, is a historic Mughal fort in Delhi, built by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648. It symbolizes India's independence.

History

Served as the Mughal capital for over 200 years. Key events include the 1857 revolt and independence speeches.

Architecture

Red sandstone walls, Diwan-e-Aam, Diwan-e-Khas, and the famous Lahori Gate. Features marble inlays and gardens.
