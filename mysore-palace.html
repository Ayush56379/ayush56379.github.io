About Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace, located in Karnataka, is one of the most magnificent royal palaces in India. It was the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and remains a symbol of Mysore’s rich heritage. The palace attracts millions of visitors every year, especially during the famous Dasara festival.

History

The original wooden palace was destroyed by fire in 1897. The current structure was built in 1912 under the supervision of British architect Henry Irwin. The palace showcases Indo-Saracenic architecture with a blend of Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic styles.

Architecture

The palace is built using gray granite and features beautiful domes, arches, and stained glass windows. Major highlights include:

  • Durbar Hall
  • Ambavilasa (Private Hall)
  • Kalyana Mantapa (Marriage Pavilion)
  • Royal Throne

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Mysore Palace is between October and March. The palace is beautifully illuminated on Sundays and public holidays.

Entry Fee & Timings

Indian Visitors: ₹70 Foreign Visitors: ₹200 Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:30 PM (Open daily)
