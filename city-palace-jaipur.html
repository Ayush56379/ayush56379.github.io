City Palace, Jaipur

Back to Home

City Palace is the royal residence of the Maharaja of Jaipur, built in 1727 by Sawai Jai Singh II. It beautifully blends Rajput and Mughal architectural styles and houses museums showcasing royal artifacts.

History

Constructed during the establishment of Jaipur city, it has remained the seat of the Kachwaha dynasty. Parts of the palace are still used by the royal family.

Architecture

The palace features pink sandstone, marble courtyards, intricate carvings, Diwan-e-Khas, Diwan-e-Aam, and beautiful decorative gates.
