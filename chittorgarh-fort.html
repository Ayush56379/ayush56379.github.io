About Chittorgarh Fort

Chittorgarh Fort is the largest fort in India and a symbol of Rajput bravery and sacrifice. It spreads over 700 acres and stands majestically in Rajasthan.

History

The fort was the capital of Mewar and witnessed several historic battles. It is famous for the legendary tales of Rani Padmini and Maharana Pratap.

Architecture

The fort includes Vijay Stambh (Victory Tower), Kirti Stambh, Rana Kumbha Palace, and beautiful temples showcasing Rajput architecture.

UNESCO Heritage Site

Chittorgarh Fort is part of the Hill Forts of Rajasthan, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Best Time to Visit

October to March is the best time to explore the fort comfortably.

Entry Fee & Timings

Indian Visitors: ₹40 Foreign Visitors: ₹600 Timings: 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM
