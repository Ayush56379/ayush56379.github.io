Chittorgarh Fort

The Largest Fort in India & Symbol of Rajput Bravery

Introduction

Chittorgarh Fort is the largest fort in India and one of the most historic forts in Rajasthan. Spread across 700 acres, this majestic hill fort represents the courage, sacrifice, and honor of Rajput warriors.

The fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of Rajput pride.

History of Chittorgarh Fort

The fort was originally built in the 7th century by the Mauryan rulers. Later it became the capital of Mewar under the Sisodia Rajputs.

Chittorgarh witnessed three major sieges — by Alauddin Khilji (1303), Bahadur Shah (1535), and Akbar (1567). Each time, the Rajput women performed Jauhar (self-sacrifice) to protect their honor.

Rani Padmini & The Legend

One of the most famous stories associated with Chittorgarh is that of Rani Padmini. According to legend, Alauddin Khilji attacked the fort after hearing about her beauty.

The story of Rani Padmini’s sacrifice remains a powerful symbol of Rajput bravery.

Architecture & Important Structures

The fort includes several historical monuments:

  • Vijay Stambh (Victory Tower) – Built by Rana Kumbha
  • Kirti Stambh – Jain tower of fame
  • Rana Kumbha Palace
  • Padmini Palace
  • Gaumukh Reservoir

The fort walls stretch over 13 kilometers and offer breathtaking views of the surrounding plains.

Travel Guide

  • Ticket Price: Approx ₹40 (Indian), ₹600 (Foreigners)
